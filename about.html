The beauty
of imperfection

I'm a multimedia designer specializing in graphic design and video production. My work is deeply influenced by industrial and brutalist aesthetics — raw materials, exposed structures, and unpolished surfaces.

Equally important to my practice is the Japanese concept of wabi-sabi: the acceptance of transience and imperfection. I believe the most compelling design work comes from embracing what's incomplete, finding character in the worn, and creating meaning through restraint.

Every project is an opportunity to strip away the unnecessary and reveal something honest underneath.
